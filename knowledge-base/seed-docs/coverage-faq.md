# AutoClaim Insurance Co. — Coverage Guidelines

Document ID: DOC-COVERAGE-2026-V1

## 1. Comprehensive Coverage

Comprehensive coverage protects the policyholder's vehicle against physical
damage **not** caused by a collision. This includes, but is not limited to:
theft, vandalism, fire, natural disasters (hurricanes, tornadoes, floods),
falling objects (trees, hail), and animal strikes (e.g., hitting a deer).

**Standard deductible:** Unless otherwise specified on the individual policy
declaration, the standard deductible for comprehensive claims is **$500**.

**Glass repair exception:** Windshield chip repairs smaller than 6 inches
carry a **$0 deductible**. Complete windshield replacement carries a **$100
deductible**.

**Exclusions:** Comprehensive coverage does not cover damage to another
person's vehicle (handled by Liability), damage from potholes or rolling
your own vehicle (handled by Collision), or wear and tear / mechanical
breakdowns / aftermarket parts exceeding $2,000 in value.

**Theft reporting:** In the event of a stolen vehicle, the policyholder must
file a formal police report within 24 hours of discovering the theft before
a claim can be processed.

## 2. Collision Coverage

A collision claim is triggered when the policyholder's covered vehicle
strikes another vehicle, strikes a stationary object (fence, guardrail,
telephone pole), or is involved in a single-car rollover accident.

**Deductibles and payouts:** The standard collision deductible is $500, but
policyholders may opt for $1,000 to lower their premium — the specific
deductible on file is always authoritative over this general guideline.
AutoClaim Co. pays for repairs up to the Actual Cash Value (ACV) of the
vehicle. If repair costs exceed 75% of the ACV, the vehicle is deemed a
"Total Loss."

**Hit-and-run incidents:** If the policyholder is the victim of a
hit-and-run, the damages are **not** covered under standard Collision or
Comprehensive coverage. Hit-and-run incidents are exclusively covered under
"Uninsured Motorist Property Damage" (UMPD) coverage — a separate add-on.

**Time limits:** Collision claims must be reported within 48 hours of the
incident.
