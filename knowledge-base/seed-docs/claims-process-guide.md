# AutoClaim Insurance Co. — Claims Process Guide

Document ID: DOC-CLAIMS-PROCESS-2026-V1

## 1. Before Filing

A claim can only be filed against a policy that is **ACTIVE**. Lapsed or
cancelled policies cannot have new claims created against them — the
policyholder must first reinstate or renew their policy.

## 2. Filing a Claim (First Notice of Loss)

To file a claim, AutoClaim IQ needs: the verified policy number, the date
the incident occurred (which cannot be in the future), and a brief
description of what happened. Filing a claim is a significant action, so
the agent always asks the policyholder to explicitly confirm before the
claim is officially submitted.

Once submitted, a claim is assigned:
- A unique claim number (format `CLM-<year>-<6 digits>`).
- A claims adjuster, assigned automatically based on current caseload
  distribution across the adjuster roster.
- An initial status of `SUBMITTED`.

## 3. Claim Status Lifecycle

Claims move through the following statuses: `SUBMITTED` → `UNDER_REVIEW` →
`APPROVED` or `DENIED` → `CLOSED`. Policyholders can ask for a status update
at any time using their claim number.

## 4. Special Cases

**Hit-and-run:** Not covered under standard Comprehensive or Collision — see
the Uninsured Motorist Property Damage (UMPD) coverage guidance in the
coverage FAQ.

**Total loss:** If repair costs exceed 75% of the vehicle's Actual Cash
Value, the adjuster will classify the claim as a total loss and the payout
process differs from a standard repair claim — the policyholder will be
contacted directly by their assigned adjuster to walk through next steps.

**Multiple claims:** A policyholder may have more than one claim on file at
a time if they were in separate, unrelated incidents.
